PURPOSE AND SCOPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This document sets out the leave and attendance framework applicable to all permanent and fixed-term employees of Meridian Facilities Group across all UAE operating locations. It replaces all prior leave guidance circulars issued before January 2026. Queries regarding interpretation should be directed to the HR Department via the MFG-HRMS portal or by contacting HR directly during business hours.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
LEAVE CATEGORIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Annual Leave

All permanent employees are entitled to 22 working days of annual leave per calendar year, accruing on a pro-rata basis from the date of joining. Annual leave balances are visible to employees and their line managers via the MFG-HRMS self-service portal. Leave balances that are not used within the calendar year may not ordinarily be carried forward without written approval from the relevant department head. In exceptional circumstances, a maximum of 5 days may be carried forward to Q1 of the following year, subject to HR Director confirmation.

Sick Leave

Employees are entitled to 15 days of paid sick leave per calendar year. For absences of a single day or two consecutive days, employees are required to notify their line manager before 9:00 AM on the first day of absence and submit a self-declaration form via the MFG-HRMS portal upon return to work. For absences of three or more consecutive days, a medical certificate from a licensed UAE healthcare provider must be submitted to HR within two working days of the employee's return. Sick leave that is not supported by the required documentation may be reclassified as unpaid leave at the discretion of HR.

Emergency Leave

Employees may request up to 3 working days of emergency leave per calendar year for unforeseen personal circumstances, including bereavement of an immediate family member, a medical emergency affecting a dependent, or a significant domestic incident. Emergency leave is granted at the discretion of the line manager and must be followed by a brief written summary of the circumstances submitted to HR within 5 working days. Emergency leave does not accumulate and unused entitlement does not carry forward.

Unpaid Leave

Employees may apply for unpaid leave for personal or professional development purposes beyond their annual entitlement. Applications must be submitted at least 20 working days in advance and require the approval of both the line manager and the department head. Unpaid leave is not guaranteed and is subject to operational requirements. HR must be informed of all approved unpaid leave arrangements prior to commencement.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUBMISSION AND NOTIFICATION PROCEDURES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Annual Leave Requests: All annual leave requests must be submitted via the MFG-HRMS portal with a minimum of 3 working days advance notice. Requests submitted with less than 3 working days notice may not be approved unless exceptional circumstances exist, at the line manager's discretion.

Sick Leave Notification: Employees who are unable to attend work due to illness must notify their line manager before 9:00 AM on the first day of absence. This notification may be by telephone call, WhatsApp message, or email, and should include an estimated return date where known. The formal sick leave record must subsequently be logged in the MFG-HRMS portal no later than the employee's first day back.

Emergency Leave Notification: Emergency leave should be communicated to the line manager as soon as practically possible. The formal request must be submitted in the MFG-HRMS system within 48 hours of the leave commencing.

All leave records are maintained centrally by HR through the MFG-HRMS system. Discrepancies between employee records and HR records should be raised with the HR Department within 10 working days of the relevant period.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
APPROVAL AUTHORITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Line managers are authorised to approve annual leave requests of up to 5 consecutive working days. Requests for annual leave exceeding 5 consecutive working days require the additional approval of the relevant department head before the request is confirmed in MFG-HRMS. Requests of 10 or more consecutive working days also require the HR Director to be notified, though HR Director approval is not required unless the employee is a department head themselves.

For department heads, all leave requests of any duration must be approved by the Operations Director.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RETURN TO WORK FOLLOWING EXTENDED SICK LEAVE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Employees returning from sick leave of 5 or more consecutive working days are required to participate in a return-to-work discussion with their line manager on their first day back. The purpose of this discussion is to confirm the employee's fitness to return, identify any temporary adjustments that may support a smooth transition, and review any outstanding work matters. A brief summary of the discussion is to be logged by the line manager in the MFG-HRMS system within 24 hours. HR may be invited to participate in the return-to-work discussion where either the employee or the line manager considers this appropriate.

Employees returning from sick leave of 15 or more consecutive working days, or from recurring absences totalling more than 20 days within a rolling 6-month period, will be referred to the HR Director for a formal attendance review. This process is supportive in nature and is designed to assist the employee and their manager in identifying sustainable arrangements.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONFIDENTIALITY NOTICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Individual leave records are treated as confidential personal information in accordance with Meridian Facilities Group data handling policies. Managers should not disclose the leave status or reason for absence of any employee to colleagues without the employee's consent. All HR records are retained securely within the MFG-HRMS system and are accessible only to authorised HR staff and relevant line management.

For queries regarding this policy document, contact the HR Department via the MFG-HRMS portal or at hr@meridian-fg.ae.
